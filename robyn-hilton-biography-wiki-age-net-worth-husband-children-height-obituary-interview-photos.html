Biography

Robyn Hilton (born 13 July 1944), sometimes called Robyn J. Molyneux, is a renowned American actress and model from the country. 

Following her debut as a supporting actor in the 1974 comedy film Blazing Saddles, she had a busy acting career in the 1970s and 1980s. She also had numerous TV appearances during that time, including The Rookies, Police Woman, and Starsky and Hutch, to mention a few. In 1985, she played her final role for a credit.

Robyn Hilton, an Idaho native, grew up playing with farm animals on a farm close to Twin Falls. She graduated from Boise Junior College after attending Jerome High School. She subsequently continued to Utah State University to complete her further studies.

The actress is still remembered for her distinctive acting abilities even if she is currently absent from Hollywood. For her contributions to the American entertainment business, Robyn Hilton continues to be regarded as a role model and an inspiration to many aspiring artists.

Early Life & Education

On July 13, 1944, in Twin Falls, Idaho, the United States, Robyn J. Molyneux was named Robyn Hilton. She comes from a Mormon household and has Scandinavian and Cherokee Indian ancestry. 

Robyn Hilton graduated from Jerome High School and afterward attended Boise Junior College to further her education. She eventually received her degree from Utah State University.

Career

In 1964, Robyn Hilton began her acting career. Initially, she played supporting roles in My Tale Is Hot and Kiss Me Quick movies. Later, in 1974, she appeared as Miss Stein in the parody Western film Blazing Saddles. She also played Denise in the movie The Single Girls and Linda Loveman in The Last Porno Flick that same year. 

The same year, she appeared in supporting roles in the TV series The Rookies and Police Woman. She then appeared in the 1975 comedy Video Vixens as a result. In the action movie Doc Savage: The Man of Bronze that same year, the actress portrayed Karen. 

Robyn Hilton appeared in the Starsky and Hutch television episode Huggy Bear and Turkey in 1977. She finally made an appearance as Maid Maria in the action-adventure movie Malibu Express in 1985 after years of inactivity.

As a model, Robyn Hilton has been featured in several magazines. She was referenced in a Playboy magazine piece titled Sex in Cinema from November 1974. She was featured in the magazine’s Sex Stars piece one month later.

Personal Life

In terms of her romantic relationships, Robyn Hilton had a number of them when she was younger. She dated Mitch Orenstein and Peter Knecht before marrying William Hilton in 1961. 

The couple divorced in 1968 after having two children together. The actress stayed single for a while after divorcing Robyn Hilton. Later, in 1990, she wed Alan Mihoces. She later divorced him as well after having one child with him.
