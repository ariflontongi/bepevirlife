Answer

A major internal struggle plagued Louis Mallard over the course of the narrative. In the short tale, she finds herself at odds with her own emotions, torn between what she should feel and how she should behave and what her genuine emotions are. When Mrs. Mallard receives the news of her husband’s death, it seems that she is despondent and grieving her husband’s loss.

 

What, in the context of this, is the external conflict in the tale of an hour to consider?

A major source of external tension for Louise Mallard is her place in society, which expects her to define herself via her marriage and submit to his wishes—and to feel devastated when she learns of his death. More importantly, she recognises that a woman has the ability to subordinate her husband to her will.

 

Second, what is the climax of a tale that takes place in an hour?

The climax of “The Story of an Hour” occurs when Mrs. Mallard starts to experience feelings of delight as a result of her newfound existence. Because her spouse is no longer living, she has the opportunity to be set free and begin over, which is represented by the arrival of spring. This is the climax because it sets the stage for what will happen to her, which is death.

Mrs. Mallard as a wife and Mrs. Mallard as a woman are both shown.
