Hold on to Your Stetsons, We're Wranglin' NBA Talent in Texas!

You might think of Texas as a land of tumbleweeds, ten-gallon hats, and maybe even a suspiciously large longhorn steer or two (looking at you, UT Austin). But hold your horses, because the Lone Star State also boasts a surprising number of NBA stars, past and present!

From Boot Scootin' to Ballin': College Ballers Turned NBA All-Stars

Texas has a rich college basketball tradition, and some of those hotshot youngsters have gone on to light up the NBA hardwood. Kevin Durant, a name that needs no introduction, spent a year cookin' up his skills at The University of Texas before becoming a scoring machine in the pros. There's also the defensive dynamo P.J. Tucker, who after a stint at the University of Texas (Austin, by the way, gotta be specific in Texas!), went on to become a champion with the Miami Heat.

Now, we can't forget the guards! T.J. Ford, with his lightning-quick speed honed at UT Austin, became a fan favorite for the Indiana Pacers. And speaking of speedy point guards, Cory Joseph, who prepped at the University of Texas, has zipped his way across the court for several teams.

More Than Just Longhorns: Texas Talent Spreads Wide

But hold your horns, Texas A&M Aggies! Don't think we forgot about you. Myles Turner, the Indiana Pacers' shot-blocking extraordinaire, developed his skills at Texas A&M before heading to the NBA.

Now, Texas is a big state, and talent isn't confined to just a couple of colleges. Mo Bamba, a rim-protecting force for the Orlando Magic, prepped at Westbury Christian School in Houston. There's also Tristan Thompson, who spent a year at the University of Texas before becoming a champion with the Cleveland Cavaliers.

This is just a taste of the Texas-sized talent pool in the NBA. From the hustle and heart of P.J. Tucker to the smooth shooting of Kevin Durant, these players prove that Texas has more to offer than just wide-open spaces (although, those are pretty great too).

So next time you're watching an NBA game, keep your eye out for those players with a little bit of Texas twang in their game. You might just see a future Hall-of-Famer who got their start under the bright lights of a Friday night high school game in the heart of Texas.
