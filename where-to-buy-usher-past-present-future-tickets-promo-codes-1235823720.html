Super Bowl viewers got it bad for Usher after his sweat-filled, star-studded Apple Music Halftime Show performance. Following the show, the eight-time Grammy-winning R&B artist announced 10 additional dates for his Past Present Future tour to support his upcoming album, Coming Home — prompting him to become the most-searched name for concert tickets and breaking fellow game attendee Taylor Swift’s record, according to StubHub.

The third-party ticketing platform reports that searches for Usher concert tickets were up five times after the Super Bowl, making him the top-searched name that day. Up until then, Swift was the most-searched artist in 2024.

Sales are now underway at Ticketmaster for select dates of Usher’s Past Present Future Tour, which kicks off Aug. 16 in Atlanta. The 60-date North American tour includes dates in Austin, Boston, Brooklyn, Chicago, Los Angeles, Miami, Philadelphia, Toronto and more cities.

Usher (né Usher Raymond IV) and his longtime girlfriend, Jennifer Goicoechea, wed in Las Vegas after the Super Bowl. Universal Studio Group’s UCP recently announced that the 45-year-old singer’s music will be brought to life in a new drama series inspired by his songs.

To celebrate the halftime headliner, Apple Music is offering a 30-day free trial to new subscribers, among other deals that let you listen to Usher’s new album and more for free. After the trial period, the subscription auto-renews at $10.99 per month; learn more and sign up here.
